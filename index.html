About the Artist

A Life in Art

With a passion for capturing the beauty found in everyday objects and the human form, my work spans still life painting, portraiture, and exquisite miniatures. Each piece is crafted with meticulous attention to detail and a deep respect for classical techniques passed down through generations.

Working from my studio, I combine traditional methods with a contemporary eye — seeking to create works that feel both timeless and alive. Whether painting the quiet drama of a still life arrangement or the subtle character of a portrait, I strive to invite the viewer into a moment of stillness and contemplation.

As President of the Royal Miniatures Society, I am dedicated to preserving and advancing the art of miniature painting — a tradition stretching back centuries that demands the utmost skill, patience, and precision.

Learn & Create

Art Workshops

Join me for intimate, hands-on workshops designed for all skill levels. Whether you're picking up a brush for the first time or looking to refine your technique, these sessions offer personalised guidance in a relaxed, inspiring environment.

From the fundamentals of colour mixing and composition to advanced glazing techniques, each workshop is tailored to help you develop confidence and discover your own artistic voice.

Still Life Painting

Master the art of observation. Learn to see and render light, shadow, and form using classical still life setups.

Portrait Drawing

Explore the techniques behind capturing likeness and character, from initial sketch to finished painting.

Miniature Painting

Discover the precision and patience of miniature painting — working small to create something extraordinary.

Colour Theory

Understand the science and poetry of colour — mixing, temperature, and the relationships that make paintings sing.

Oil Techniques

From alla prima to glazing, explore the full range of oil painting methods used by the Old Masters and today's finest painters.

Watercolour Foundations

Embrace the unpredictable beauty of watercolour. Learn to work with the medium, not against it.
